Travel Tips

Here are some travel tips to stay safe while on your trip:

Lock Up Your Valuables

When traveling, you may want or need to bring valuable items with you. This could be your social security card, birth certificate, or other sensitive documents. You may also be bringing various forms of money, as well as other expensive items like cameras or laptops. While you are traveling you want to avoid theft. Ideally, this would mean limiting the number of valuable items you bring, and only taking what is absolutely necessary. It is important to lock up any items that you do decide to bring, most likely in a hotel safe or locker. Stay vigilant to keep your items safe!

Know Emergency Information for the Area

It is crucial to know the contact information for local emergency services wherever you are visiting. While 911 is the emergency number throughout the United States, if you are traveling abroad, other countries have different emergency phone numbers. Additionally, if you are calling for emergency services within the United States, it is also important that you know the name of the area you are visiting, in order to effectively communicate with the operators. You may also want to scan copies of important documents into a flash drive that you bring, in case any of your documents get lost or stolen. Emergency preparedness is crucial!

Research Popular Scams

In popular tourist areas, scams are relatively common. The best way to protect yourself from scammers is to research popular scams in the area before you visit. Simply reaching out to others who have visited the city or using a Google search can help you defend yourself from scams before you even arrive.

Share Your Plans with a Friend or Family Member

When making travel plans it is always smart to share them with someone you trust--either a friend or family member. If you plan to have a detailed itinerary, email or text it to that person. If you donâ€™t have an absolute travel plan, simply checking in with that person regularly is good as well. This ensures that someone knows where you should be in case of an emergency in the area you are visiting, so they can contact the proper authorities. Informing someone of your plans will also enable them to make sure you return when planned. This will keep you safe!

Avoid Using Public Wifi

Public WiFi, although convenient, can be very risky to use. It is much easier for hackers to steal important information, such as bank and credit card information, when using public WiFi. If you must use public WiFi on your trip, look into using a virtual private network (VPN) to securely use the Internet.
